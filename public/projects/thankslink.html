ThanksLink Panel UI - JPN Partner

04.2024

ThanksLink is a travel reservation platform that offers insurance products. With an expressed interest in hosting Travel Guard TI, ThanksLink would like to serve a standard insurance panel for various umbrella partners.

Duration:

2 Months (intermittent)

Team:

Myself & offshore resources

Functon:

UI Design, Web Design, CM

Overview

Partnered with the business side of ThanksLink, I receive general mockups of the expected content within the panel. This is an openHTML setup, with pre-existing design and front end markup that was already approved by the business. I was tasked with designing a new panel UI to have in our back pocket for demoing the possibilities of optimization/consultation, and showcasing our team's out-of-the-box capabilities.

Process

My initial feeling on the ThanksLink mockup/requirement document(s) is the textual content of the panel a much higher priority than the styling/feeling of its messaging. I outlined 4 general areas for improving the panel content based on experiences with other partners.

UI Improvement Assumptions

Example mockup with my annotations

  • Consider changing the heading content/layout

  • Consider different images or image size/shape

  • Consider reorganizing or restyling content to draw attention to the important information

  • Consider styling the radio buttons differently

Other Considerations

Dyamic Content

Are there dynamic text or handlebars being implemented in the panel? Information pertaining to age, names, or values will likely be dynamic and unique depending on travel details.

Panel Implementation

How is the panel being implemented on the website? How much real estate does the panel have on the page?
